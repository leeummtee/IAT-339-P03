Poster

Designing a poster to communicate a piece of advice to incoming freshmen.

summary

Individual two-week project where students had to design posters for SIAT advisors to use when welcoming SIAT freshmen.

programs used

Adobe Illustrator

1. sketches

My design process started out with multiple ideation sketches for a large banner-style poster with tips on getting started with Discord. Since we were required by the teaching staff to incorporate signs and symbols, I decided to make a design with the Discord logo wearing a cape to symbolize it as a powerful tool that can save the day.

2. initial renders

Since we were required by the teaching staff to incorporate signs and symbols, I decided to make a design with the Discord logo wearing a cape to symbolize it as a powerful tool that can save the day. After creating these sketches, I then made a higher fidelity mock-up in Adobe Illustrator. I also ended up placing Discord’s name on a shooting star to symbolize how it can be the answer to students’ wishes for an easier way to communicate on projects online.

3. final product

My initial feedback from my peers was that a banner-style poster with so much information would be overwhelming for students to look at. Based on their critiques, I decided to shrink it down to an A0 size (841mm x 1189mm) by cutting out the steps and replacing it with a summary of Discord’s features.

4. reflection

Working on this project made me realize how important it is for a poster to able to get a lot of information across with just a quick glance. My first design had too much content and would require a passerby to thoroughly read through all of it to get the full meaning behind the poster. However, I feel that the changes I made allowed for a much clearer message to be communicated in a much quicker way.
